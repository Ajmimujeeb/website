The Kite Runner

Author: Khaled Hosseini

Description: "The Kite Runner" by Khaled Hosseini is a powerful and poignant novel that explores themes of guilt, redemption, friendship, and the complex nature of human relationships. Set against the backdrop of a changing Afghanistan, the story follows the life of Amir, a young boy from a wealthy family in Kabul, and his complicated friendship with Hassan, the son of his father's servant. The novel begins in the 1970s, during Amir's childhood, where he and Hassan are inseparable, spending their days playing together and participating in kite-flying tournaments. However, a tragic incident during one such tournament becomes a turning point, leading Amir to betray Hassan in a moment of cowardice. This act of betrayal haunts Amir into adulthood, especially after he and his father flee to the United States following the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan.
